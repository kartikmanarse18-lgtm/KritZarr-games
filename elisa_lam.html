ELISA LAM
A True Crime Investigation

She Checked Into a Hotel She Never Checked Out

"The water had a funny taste," the guests said. For three weeks, they showered in it. Brushed their teeth with it. Drank it. None of them knew that 21-year-old Elisa Lam was floating naked in the rooftop tank, her body decomposing in 1,000 gallons of darkness.

It began with a young woman seeking adventure. Elisa Lam, a 21-year-old Canadian student with a bright smile and a love for travel, arrived at the Cecil Hotel in downtown Los Angeles on January 28, 2013. The hotel was cheap, historic, and—for those who knew its secrets—deeply cursed. Built in the 1920s, it had hosted serial killers, witnessed suicides, and accumulated roughly 80 deaths in its decaying walls. But to a budget traveler, it was just a place to sleep.

Within days, Elisa's behavior became erratic. Her roommates complained she was leaving strange notes—"go away," "go home"—and locking them out of their shared room. The hotel moved her to a private room. On January 31, her last day alive, she visited The Last Bookstore, where staff remembered her as chatty and friendly. Back at the Cecil, employees saw her wandering the halls. She shouted in the lobby: "I'm crazy, but so is L.A.!" Then she walked toward the elevators—and vanished.

For nineteen days, no one could find her. Her parents reported her missing. Police searched with dogs. Flyers went up across the city. Then, on February 13, the LAPD released a surveillance video that would haunt the internet forever: 2.5 minutes of Elisa in an elevator, pressing buttons frantically, hiding in corners, making gestures at invisible walls. The timestamp was blurred. Footage appeared missing. The world watched, and the world theorized.

But the horror was just beginning. For nearly three weeks, hotel guests had complained about low water pressure and discolored, foul-tasting water. Some described it as black. On February 19, maintenance worker Santiago Lopez climbed to the roof to inspect the water tanks. One hatch was open. Looking inside, he discovered Elisa's naked body floating face-up, her clothes and room key at the bottom of the tank. The water that guests had been drinking, showering in, and brushing their teeth with had been slowly absorbing her remains.

THE QUESTION THAT HAUNTS EVERYONE:
How did a 21-year-old woman, alone and in distress, navigate a locked, alarmed rooftop, climb a 10-foot ladder to a water tank, open a heavy industrial lid, and drown inside—while leaving no evidence of trauma, no signs of struggle, and no trace of her phone? The coroner ruled it an accident. The internet called it a cover-up. Some called it something darker entirely.

Case Timeline

Every known movement and event from Elisa's arrival at the Cecil Hotel to the discovery of her body.

Arrival at the Cecil Hotel

Elisa checks into the Cecil Hotel in downtown LA. Originally assigned a shared room, she is moved to a private room (Room 506) after roommates complain about her erratic behavior—leaving notes saying "go away" and locking them out.

January 31, 2013
Last Known Day Alive

Elisa visits The Last Bookstore where staff describe her as outgoing. Back at the Cecil, employees see her wandering. She shouts "I'm crazy, but so is L.A.!" in the lobby, is found in a restricted area, then walks toward the elevators.

February 1, 2013
Reported Missing

Elisa fails to check out and misses her trip to Santa Cruz. Her parents report her missing to LAPD. Police search with K-9 units but find nothing. Her scent trail is lost near a window leading to a fire escape.

February 13, 2013
Elevator Video Released

LAPD releases 2.5 minutes of elevator surveillance. The video shows Elisa behaving erratically—pressing multiple buttons, hiding, making strange gestures. Timestamp is blurred and nearly a minute appears missing. Goes viral globally.

February 19, 2013
Body Discovered

After nearly three weeks of guest complaints about low water pressure and black water, maintenance worker Santiago Lopez discovers Elisa's naked body floating in a rooftop water tank. The hatch is open. Her clothes, watch, and room key are at the bottom.

February 21, 2013
Autopsy Performed

LA County Coroner's Office performs autopsy. Findings: No physical trauma, no sexual assault, no illicit drugs, negligible alcohol (0.02g%), very low bipolar medication levels. Traces of ibuprofen and Sinutab found.

June 20, 2013
Official Ruling

Death ruled "Accidental Drowning" with "Bipolar Disorder" as significant contributing factor. Coroner states no evidence of foul play. Police believe she accessed roof via fire escape, climbed 10-foot ladder, and entered tank voluntarily.

Physical Evidence

The Water Tank

Four 1,000-gallon cisterns on the Cecil Hotel roof. Elisa was found in the easternmost tank. The hatch was found open. Tanks were 8ft tall, 4ft diameter, perched on concrete blocks. No fixed ladder—portable ladder required.

Missing Evidence

The Missing Phone

Elisa's phone was never recovered—not with her body, not in her room, not anywhere in the hotel. This remains one of the most puzzling aspects. If she drowned accidentally, where is her phone?

Crime Scene Photo

Rooftop Access

The Cecil Hotel roof was supposed to be secured with locked doors and alarms. However, a fire escape provided alternative access. Police dogs searched the roof but did not check inside the water tanks during initial search.

Anomaly 1

The Missing Minute

Viewers noticed the timestamp appears to jump erratically. The elevator doors seem about to close before the video jumps to a later point. LAPD and hotel manager Amy Price denied tampering, stating raw tapes were handed directly to investigators. Some speculate footage was removed to protect another person's identity.

Anomaly 2

Obscured Timestamp

The timestamp at the bottom of the video is deliberately blurred and obscured, making it impossible to verify the exact time of recording or confirm if segments were removed. This fueled conspiracy theories about video manipulation.

Behavioral Analysis

Expert Interpretation

Forensic pathologist Dr. Jason Tovar and clinical psychologist Dr. Judy Ho concluded her behavior is consistent with a severe psychotic episode: disorganized thinking, paranoia, hallucinations. Her hand movements suggest she may be feeling invisible walls or warding off perceived threats.

Technical

Elevator Mechanics

The elevator doors remain open for an unusually long time. Some theorize the "hold door" button was pressed, or someone outside was holding the door. Others suggest the elevator was malfunctioning. The Cecil Hotel was known for broken elevators.

Legal Document

Wrongful Death Lawsuit

Filed by Elisa's parents in September 2013 against the Cecil Hotel. Argued management failed to secure hazardous areas like rooftop water tanks. Dismissed in 2015. Judge ruled hotel not liable for accident resulting from her own actions.

Police Statement

LAPD Press Release

February 19, 2013: "The body of a female was found inside one of the water tanks on the roof of the Cecil Hotel. The body has been identified as Elisa Lam... Preliminary investigation indicates no evidence of foul play."

Witness Statement

Santiago Lopez (Maintenance)

The maintenance worker who discovered the body. He climbed to the roof to investigate guest complaints about water quality. Found the tank hatch open. Discovered Elisa's naked body floating face-up. Her clothing, watch, and room key were at the bottom of the tank.

EVIDENCE

Autopsy Summary - Key Findings

External Examination
Moderate decomposition. No evidence of trauma, cuts, bruises, or injuries. No signs of sexual assault. Body found naked in water tank—clothing, watch, and room key at bottom of tank.
Toxicology
No illicit drugs. Ethanol 0.02 g%. Prescribed medications at sub-therapeutic levels. Traces of ibuprofen and Sinutab.
Internal Examination
Subcutaneous pooling of blood in anal area. Prolapsed rectum (possibly decomposition-related). Lungs consistent with drowning.
Conclusion
CAUSE: Drowning | MANNER: Accident | OTHER SIGNIFICANT CONDITION: Bipolar Disorder
